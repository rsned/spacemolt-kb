NameTypeDescription
☀ Procyon A Sun A white-yellow star slightly brighter than Sol. The system was surveyed as a prime colony candidate during the expansion era. The survey was right — the investment just never came.
⬡ Procyon Colonial Station Station A functioning station that's been 'temporarily underfunded' for longer than most pilots have been alive. The repair bay works but uses salvaged parts. The market is stocked but the selection is limited. The refueling depot charges Confederacy rates for fuel that arrives on a schedule best described as 'eventually.' Procyon has the resources and the location to be a major system. It just needs someone to invest in it.
☁ Procyon Gas Cloud Gas Cloud Hydrogen and argon harvested by an aging collection fleet. The harvesters are older than most of their operators and require creative maintenance. They still work, because Procyonites don't give up on things.
● Procyon I Planet Partially terraformed colony world. The equatorial settlements are functional but dated — infrastructure from the expansion era maintained on a declining budget. The decommissioned long-range antenna array on the northern continent 'hasn't been needed in years,' according to the official statement nobody believes.
● Procyon II Planet Industrial world where the factories still run but the workforce has been shrinking for decades. Young Procyonites leave for Nova Terra or Sol. The ones who stay say they're loyal. The ones who left say they're stubborn.
● Procyon III Planet Frozen outer world with mothballed mining installations. The equipment is maintained on a rotating schedule by a crew of twelve, just in case the Confederacy ever funds the extraction program that was approved and then defunded three times.
❄ Procyon Ice Fields Ice Field Rich ice deposits — water, nitrogen, deuterium, and helium. Six resources that represent unrealized potential. The Confederacy's resource assessment rated Procyon's ice fields as 'strategically significant.' The budget allocation rated them as 'deferred.'
