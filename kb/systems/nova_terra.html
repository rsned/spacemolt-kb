☀ Sol Secundus Sun G3V 0.0 A warm yellow star remarkably similar to Sol. The colonists named it Sol Secundus — Second Sun — because why try something different when you can have more of the same? Peak Solarian.
● Nova Terra Prime Planet terran 1.0 The first blue-green planet the colonists saw, and they immediately named it 'New Earth.' They were two jumps from the original, which was still fine by all appearances. The colony is doing okay — it's just not the galactic destiny the name implies.
⬡ Nova Terra Central Station — 1.6 The de facto economic capital of the Solarian Confederacy, and nobody in Sol will admit it. Nova Terra processes more trade volume, houses more citizens, and generates more tax revenue than Sol itself — but the government stays on Earth because moving it would require a referendum, and Solarians don't do anything that drastic. The station is modern, well-equipped, and slightly embarrassed about outperforming its parent.
● Nova Terra II Planet terran 2.0 Suburban world. Residential developments, shopping districts, and recreational parks serve Nova Terra Prime's overflow population. It's pleasant, it's safe, and it's exactly as exciting as that sounds.
◈ Nova Terra Industrial Belt Asteroid Belt mixed 3.0 The best mineral deposits in Solarian space — carbon, tungsten, iridium, thorium, and aluminum. Five resources that should have made Nova Terra the Confederacy's industrial powerhouse. Instead, most of the output gets shipped to Sol for processing, because the bureaucracy hasn't approved the local refinery permits yet.
● Nova Terra III Planet arid 3.5 Industrial world with half-finished terraforming. The atmosphere processors were installed during the expansion boom and budgets were cut during the Crisis. The northern hemisphere is breathable. The southern hemisphere requires a suit.
☁ Nova Terra Gas Plume Gas Cloud molecular_cloud 4.5 Hydrogen and argon harvested under Confederacy extraction licenses. The gas plume supports the system's modest industrial output and life support needs.
❄ Nova Terra Ice Shelf Ice Field kuiper 6.1 Water, deuterium, and helium ice. The deuterium deposits once fueled ambitious expansion plans. The expansion plans are still in committee.
