Points of Interest (8)
NameTypeDescription
☀ Hearthfire Sun A warm yellow star, unusual for Crimson space. The locals call it Hearthfire — the one concession to comfort in an empire built on iron. The Pact's civilians chose this system for its habitable zone.
◈ Ironhearth Fields Asteroid Belt Rich mineral fields supplying both the local population and the war machine. Carbon, vanadium, platinum, and palladium — self-sufficient by design.
● Ironhearth II Planet Agricultural world. Hydroponic farms and livestock operations feed the system's population. Crimson agriculture is as regimented as everything else — crop rotations run on military schedules.
● Ironhearth III Planet Gravity 1.3 standard. The Pact uses it for physical conditioning. Recruits who survive a rotation here don't complain about station gravity again.
❄ Ironhearth Ice Ring Ice Field Nitrogen ice ring at the system's outer edge. Cracked and processed for atmospheric maintenance across the system's habitats.
● Ironhearth Prime Planet The closest thing the Crimson Pact has to a homeworld. Three billion people live here — technically all enlisted, practically a functioning civilization. Schools teach metallurgy alongside weapons drill, and the harvest festivals celebrate ore yields.
⬡ Ironhearth Station Station The hearth the war machine comes home to. Behind the military docking protocols and weapons inspections, Ironhearth is the Pact's most human place — families live here, children are born here, and veterans retire here. The market stocks food and clothing alongside ammunition, and the cantina pours something that isn't technically regulation but nobody's been court-martialed for it yet.
☁ Ironhearth Thermal Vents Gas Cloud Hydrogen and xenon vent from geological activity on Ironhearth Prime's largest moon. The gas harvests supplement the population's energy needs.
